Research Interests

Traffic flow theory, behavior modeling, CAVs, simulation, machine learning, and computer vision for safer, more efficient, and resilient transportation systems.

  • Traffic flow theory and modeling: macroscopic and microscopic analysis, stream and congestion modeling, dynamic assignment, demand estimation, network optimization, real-time prediction.
  • ADS and CAV: data-driven behavior and optimization, cooperative technologies, deep RL, multi-sensor fusion including LiDAR, simulation and field testing, communication modeling (OMNeT++).
  • Microscopic simulation and ABM: heterogeneous traffic, agent-based driver/vehicle behavior, multi-modal interactions, management strategies, virtual test environments.
  • ML and computer vision for transportation data: state estimation, behavior and congestion modeling, video-based detection, trajectory analysis, multi-source fusion, real-time analytics.
  • Safety and operations: VRU behavior, pedestrian and cyclist safety, crash risk, incident detection, signal optimization, efficient mobility.
